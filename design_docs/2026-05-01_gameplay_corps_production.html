1. Purpose & Method

The 2026-04-30 doc settled the direction: cyberpunk reskin, four-corp asymmetric co-op, shared tech tree, interference mechanics. This doc is the next layer down — concrete answers to "what does each corp build, what does each corp do moment-to-moment, and what's the first end-to-end chain we want to prove."

The driving question
If we sat four players down today, what is the smallest playable slice that lets each of them do their corp's job, depend on the others, and produce one finished drink end-to-end?

How to read the tags

  • SET — design call made; folded into the slice unless re-opened.
  • SEED — starting idea worth challenging.
  • TBD — explicitly empty; needs a call.
  • Q — open question for the next session.

2. General Principles

Cross-cutting rules that apply to every corp and every production chain. These are the connective tissue that makes the four-corp loop a system rather than four parallel games.

Most buildings need water and electricity
Especially industrial. A building without its utilities connected is placed but inert. Logistics is the gatekeeper of "can this thing actually run."
Every industrial recipe consumes an agri input
No industrial activity exists in a vacuum. Even the simplest recipe pulls a raw resource from a field. This is the structural reason agri can never be skipped.
Every industrial building produces waste
Two waste types: biological waste (returnable to agri as fertilizer — closing a loop) and sewage / chemical waste (must be piped away by logistics). Pollution is the long-term consequence of poor handling and slowly shrinks agri-suitable land.
Pollution reshapes the map over time
At session start, most of the map is agri-suitable. As industry produces, a creeping unsuitability spreads. This converts the early-game "where do I plant?" into a late-game "is there anywhere left to plant?" — a natural escalation curve without scripted events.
Suitability is visualized as a heat map
Tropico-style. Agri toggles a heat-map overlay per crop type; tiles tint by suitability. The same overlay system can later show pollution, water access, or power coverage for the other corps.
Two-layer tech: per-corp internal + shared higher-level
Each corp has its own internal tech tree of boosts and modifiers to what it already owns (yield, efficiency, building upgrades). The shared higher-level tree unlocks new stages that affect everyone (new production chains, new building types, new vehicle tiers). See Section 6.
Machines are reused across drink types where logical
A fermenter is a fermenter. Where the real-world process overlaps, the machine asset and recipe slot overlap too. This keeps the machine roster manageable and rewards players for reading the recipe family tree. Needs research pass: which machines are actually shared between beer / cider / spirits / wine.

3. What Each Corp Can Do

For each corp: the verbs the player executes, the buildings they place, what they manage, and their MVP action for the first slice.

Corp 01 · Agricultural

Grows the inputs. Tends the land.

The soil-and-suitability puzzle. The corp closest to the land and the first to feel the cost of pollution from elsewhere.

Core verbs SET

  • Place the main farm building (water connection point — logistics hooks pipes here)
  • Place fields (generic farmland tiles; player chooses crop)
  • Place orchards (after first agri unlock)
  • Toggle the suitability heat map per crop type
  • Tend fields by hand: provide water, nutrients, monitor soil pH (manual at first, automatable later)
  • Place silos for grain/fruit temporary storage

Buildings on day one SET

  • Main farm building — required, water terminus
  • Field tile — generic; crop chosen per-field at placement (barley or hops at start)
  • Silo — grain/fruit buffer storage; logistics pickup point

Crops & orchards SET

  • Start of game: barley, hops
  • First agri unlock: orchards · apples (feeds early-game cider, later calvados and other apple spirits)
  • Each crop demands a different soil type — heat map exposes the choice

What the player manages

  • Soil suitability: per-tile, per-crop. Tropico-style overlay.
  • Water: incoming from logistics water mains via main farm building
  • Nutrients & pH: hand-tended at first; could be a field-tending minigame that gives the player something to do between expansions SEED
  • Pollution encroachment: watch the suitability map shrink as industry grows

Internal tech (later)

  • Yield boosts, faster growth, better water efficiency
  • Drones & robotics — automate the manual field-tending
  • Pollution-resistant crop variants

Open question for agri

Q Should agri be allowed to build the simplest dirt roads for its own internal logistics (silo→farm→field), leaving logistics corp to handle everything inter-industry? Leaning yes — gives agri a self-contained loop without making logistics babysit every silo.

MVP action for the first slice

Place main farm building, two fields (barley + hops), watch the heat map, harvest into silos, hand off to logistics.

Corp 02 · Industrial

Turns inputs into product. Owns recipes.

Places production buildings on the strategic map and steps inside them to design machine layouts. Each building is a recipe family; each machine slot is a step in that recipe.

Core verbs SET

  • Place a production building on the strategic map
  • Step inside its interior (top-down 20×20)
  • Lay down machines + connections to define the active recipe
  • Connect input ports (logistics delivers raw materials here)
  • Connect waste output: biological waste → fertilizer pickup; sewage → logistics pipe

Buildings on day one SET

  • Malthouse — water-pipe required; runs the barley-malt recipe; upgradeable to handle multiple recipes simultaneously
  • Generic brewery — water-pipe required; first chain is lager beer (see Section 4)

Recipes SET

  • Start: barley malt (in malthouse) · lager beer (in brewery)
  • Malthouse upgrades unlock more malt recipes and let one malthouse run multiple in parallel
  • First industrial unlock: cider chain (apples → cider, in a brewery building)
  • Probably needs a new brewery building per chain rather than one brewery hot-swapping recipes SEED — keeps interior layouts stable and visually legible

Machines

  • Basic machinery available at start; advanced machinery later via tech
  • Machines are reused across drink types where the real process actually overlaps (fermenters, milling, filtration). Needs a research pass to map the actual overlap.

What the player manages

  • Recipe selection & machine layout inside each building
  • Input pacing matched to logistics delivery cadence
  • Waste streams: biological → fertilizer (closes loop with agri); chemical/sewage → logistics piping
  • Building utilities: water + power on, or building is inert

Internal tech (later)

  • Throughput boosts, recipe efficiency, lower waste output
  • Machine upgrades (faster, cleaner, multi-recipe)
  • Building expansions (bigger interior footprint)

MVP action for the first slice

Receive barley → malthouse produces malt → brewery combines malt + hops + water → outputs lager beer at loading bay. Waste handling can start minimal in slice 1.

Corp 03 · Logistics

Connects everything. Owns infrastructure.

Without logistics, no other corp can interact. Owns water, sewage, power, roads, and vehicle routing — utilities and transport rolled together (see Q1, now resolved).

Core verbs SET

  • Lay water mains to buildings that need them (agri farms, industrial buildings)
  • Lay sewage pipes from industrial waste outputs
  • Lay power lines (or skip if roads carry power — see below)
  • Build roads; roads can carry power, Cities Skylines 2 style SEED
  • Place vehicles and configure routes in a node-based view (OpenTTD's logic, but visualized as a node graph rather than a list/table)

Buildings & infrastructure on day one SET

  • Water main segment + pumping station
  • Sewage pipe segment
  • Road tile (basic; carries power)
  • Truck depot · 1 truck

Routing model SEED

Old-school transport-tycoon route lists are brutal to read. Instead: a node graph view where each pickup/dropoff is a node, vehicles are edges with assigned cargo. Reads at a glance, scales with the network.

What logistics moves

  • Agri silos → industrial input bays
  • Industrial output → next industrial building (inter-industry)
  • Industrial output → business sales/trade points
  • Industrial biological waste → agri (fertilizer loop)

Internal tech (later)

  • New road tiers · larger / faster vehicles
  • Rail / monorail
  • Drone / aerial logistics (fits the cyberpunk theme cleanly)
  • Pipeline efficiency, smaller substations

MVP action for the first slice

Connect water mains (source → farm, source → malthouse, source → brewery), lay roads silo → malthouse → brewery → sales outlet, configure one truck route on the node graph.

Corp 04 · Economic / Marketing

Sells the product. Holds the wallet. Brokers between corps.

Spatial presence (sales outlets, trade depots) plus the financial layer that ties all four corps together.

Core verbs SET

  • Place selling points at suitable locations (cities, towns, outposts)
  • Place trading depots and set up trade routes with third parties
  • Manage loans, monitor income / expenditure tables
  • Move credits between players on demand
  • Configure auto-income percentages per corp (default X% each, adjustable) — so other corps have working capital to expand
  • Run ad campaigns and sales boosts
  • Communicate market demand changes to other corps so they can adapt production

Buildings on day one SET

  • Sales outlet (one — bar / kiosk equivalent at a nearby town)
  • Trading depot TBD — could be slice-2 if too much for first session

Demand model

  • Early game: demand isn't a constraint — sell everything you make
  • Later: demand shifts by region, drink type, season — business has to read the market and tell the others "switch some breweries to cider"

Trade & logistics handoff

  • Trading routes need logistics infrastructure (roads, vehicles) — business books the route, logistics provides the wheels
  • This forces ongoing business ↔ logistics dialogue (good — it's another co-op pressure point)

Internal tech (later)

  • Better ad-campaign efficiency, larger sales-radius outlets
  • More favorable loan terms, larger trade contracts
  • Market-intelligence upgrades (forecasting, demand previews)

MVP action for the first slice

Place one sales outlet, set the auto-income split across corps, watch credits flow when beer arrives. Loans, ads, trading depots can wait.

Cross-corp consistency check
The four MVP actions above interlock into one chain: field → silo → malthouse → brewery → sales outlet, with logistics threading water, power, roads, and trucks through every link, and business setting the price and routing the credits back. If any of the four can't do their MVP without help from another, that's the co-op-essential design proving itself in the smallest possible slice.

4. First Production Chain — Lager Beer

Lager is the slice-1 chain. Reasons: malthouse and brewery sprites already exist; barley field is animated; grain-to-beer is the cleanest pipeline in the drinks space; everyone understands what beer is. (Detailed brewing reference on the project's Miro page.)

Recipe (set)

Lager Beer ←
+ Malt ← Barley (Field, Agri)
+ Hops (Field, Agri)
+ Water (main, Logistics)

Later beers introduce different grains and hop varieties — same recipe shape, different inputs. The chain is a template.

Stages on the map

Stage 1 · Agri
Barley field + Hops field on suitable tiles → harvested into silos
Stage 2 · Logistics
Truck pickup at silos → deliver barley to malthouse; deliver hops directly to brewery
Stage 3 · Industrial A
Malthouse (water-fed): barley → malt
Stage 4 · Logistics
Truck pickup at malthouse → deliver malt to brewery
Stage 5 · Industrial B
Brewery (water + power-fed): malt + hops + water → lager beer; biological waste branches off; sewage piped out
Stage 6 · Logistics
Truck pickup at brewery → deliver lager beer to sales outlet
Stage 7 · Business
Sales outlet: lager beer → credits flow to shared pool, split per the auto-income config

What the chain teaches each corp

CorpWhat slice 1 teaches themWhat it deliberately doesn't
AgriHeat map, two crops on suitable tiles, manual tending, hand-off to siloOrchards, pollution-shrunk land, automation, fertilizer loop
IndustrialPlace malthouse + brewery, basic machine layout, water/power dependencyMulti-recipe malthouse, byproduct-heavy chains, advanced machinery
LogisticsWater mains, roads (carrying power), one truck on a node-based routeSewage piping, rail, drones, multi-modal optimization
BusinessPlace sales outlet, set auto-income split, watch credits arriveLoans, ad campaigns, demand shifts, trading depots, third-party trade

First unlocks (slice 2 preview)

  • Agri: orchards · apples
  • Industrial: cider chain (apples → cider) — likely a new brewery building dedicated to cider rather than hot-swapping the lager brewery
  • Eventually: apple spirits (calvados) once distillation infrastructure unlocks
Why lager first, not whisky/spirits
Distillation adds aging/maturation timing, water purity sensitivity, higher byproduct, and a meaningfully longer feedback loop. Lager is a clean fermentation pipeline that closes in minutes, not seasons. Save spirits for after the loop is proven.

6. Tech Tree Structure

The 2026-04-30 doc described a single shared tech tree. Refining that into a two-layer model:

Layer 1 — Per-corp internal tech

Each corp has its own internal progression that boosts or modifies what it already has. No new gameplay verbs introduced; just better numbers and quality-of-life.

  • Agri: yield, water efficiency, soil tolerance, eventual drone/robotics automation of field tending
  • Industrial: machine throughput, waste reduction, recipe efficiency, building expansion
  • Logistics: vehicle speed/capacity, pipe efficiency, road durability, route-planning aids
  • Business: ad efficiency, loan terms, market intelligence, sales-outlet radius

Internal tech is paid out of that corp's own balance sheet and never blocks the others. It's the corp's personal growth track.

Layer 2 — Shared higher-level tech

The communal tree from the 2026-04-30 doc. Unlocks new stages that affect every corp at once: new production chains (cider, spirits, wine), new building tiers, new vehicle classes, theme-changing reskins. Funded from shared credits, requires negotiation.

Why two layers
A single shared tree means every player has to negotiate every upgrade — eventually exhausting. A pure per-corp tree means players grow alone — losing the co-op pressure. Two layers gives each player a personal progression for steady dopamine, while the shared tree remains the table-level decision and the source of headline unlocks.

7. The First 10 Minutes of a Session

A loose script for slice 1, updated against the concrete design above.

  1. 0:00 — Players pick corps. Map loads. Agri toggles the barley heat map and sees most of the map glowing green.
  2. 0:30 — Agri places main farm building. Two fields placed nearby, one set to barley, one to hops. Both show "needs water."
  3. 1:00 — Logistics places a water source pump and starts running mains toward agri's farm and the spots industrial is hovering over.
  4. 2:00 — Industrial places malthouse and brewery, shows "needs water + power" until logistics catches up.
  5. 2:30 — Business places one sales outlet at the nearest town and opens the auto-income panel; sets default split.
  6. 3:30 — Logistics finishes water mains; industrial buildings come online. Roads laid silo → malthouse → brewery → sales outlet (carrying power as they go).
  7. 5:00 — Agri tends fields manually (provides nutrients, watches pH); first harvest into silos.
  8. 6:00 — Logistics configures one truck route on the node graph: silo → malthouse → brewery → sales outlet.
  9. 7:00 — Industrial steps inside the malthouse, lays the minimal machine line; same for the brewery.
  10. 8:00–10:00 — First malt produced, first lager brewed, first crate delivered. Credits hit the shared pool. Round of high-fives. End of slice 1 success state.
Failure modes to watch in playtest
  • Any corp finishes setup in 30 seconds and sits idle for 9 minutes — that corp's MVP is too thin.
  • Two corps need to make the same decision and don't know who decides — ownership unclear.
  • One corp blocks all three others — leverage is fine; single point of failure isn't.
  • Heat map / suitability is too subtle — agri places fields anywhere and never engages with the system.
  • The node-based route view confuses logistics more than it helps — fall back to a hybrid view if needed.

8. Open Questions

Resolved since the previous draft SET

  • Q1 (was: utilities corp split?) — Resolved: logistics owns water, sewage, power, and roads. Roads can carry power (Cities Skylines 2 style).
  • Q3 (was: business spatial presence?) — Resolved: yes, business places sales outlets and trading depots; it is not a UI-only corp.

Still open

Q1. Should agri build its own simplest dirt roads for internal logistics?
Leaning yes — agri runs silo↔farm↔field on dirt roads it owns; logistics handles every inter-industry connection. Keeps logistics from babysitting every farm tile, but adds a road-system to agri's plate.
Q2. Field-tending minigame — what is it actually?
Provides "something to do" between expansions. Could be a clicker for water/nutrient/pH, a small layout puzzle, or a periodic alert system. Need to nail the scope before art commits to it. Risks becoming busywork.
Q3. Are corp sub-types picked at session start, or unlocked through the shared tech tree?
Pick-at-start gives players identity from minute 0. Tech-tree unlock keeps slice 1 simpler. Carries over from previous draft.
Q4. Is recipe layout inside an industrial building required in slice 1, or can it auto-resolve?
Required layout is honest to the design but expands scope. Auto-resolve gets us to "first beer" faster. Could ship slice 1 with auto-resolve and introduce real layout in slice 2.
Q5. New brewery building per chain, or one brewery hot-swaps recipes?
Per-chain building keeps interior layouts stable, makes the map readable, and gives art clear silhouettes. Hot-swap is more efficient on real estate. Lean is per-chain — but worth a call.
Q6. What's the loss / failure state in slice 1?
If there is no fail state, slice 1 is a sandbox demo — fine for prototype. Worth deciding before playtest so feedback is calibrated.
Q7. Where does pollution / the suitability shrink first show up — slice 1 or slice 2?
Pollution is core to the design. Showing nothing in slice 1 risks the slice looking like solitaire-with-extra-steps. Showing too much in slice 1 confuses the tutorial. Probably: visible but inactive — heat map is on, pollution stays at zero in slice 1.
Q8. Default auto-income split percentages?
Equal 25/25/25/25? Weighted toward agri/industrial early because they're capital-hungry? This is a tuning question, but the default sets the tone of "who's expected to grow first."
Q9. Machine-overlap research pass — what is actually shared between drink types?
Beer, cider, wine, spirits all involve fermentation but diverge sharply at distillation, aging, carbonation. Need a real-world pass before locking the machine list. Could be a weekend research task before slice 2.
Q10. Roads-as-powerlines — adopt CS2 model directly or hybrid?
CS2 model: every road carries power. Hybrid: only certain road tiers carry power, dedicated power lines for higher capacity. Hybrid gives logistics a real progression on the power side; pure CS2 keeps things simpler.

9. Scratch / Parking Lot

Half-formed thoughts
  • Could the brewery interior double as a tutorial space — first time you enter, ghosts show you where to place machines?
  • Cyberpunk reskin opportunity: barley field → synth-grain vat later. Slice 1 stays barley to keep art reuse.
  • Narrative event hook: "regulator inspection — first crate of beer must arrive by minute 10" — converts the success state into a beat instead of a quiet milestone.
  • Per-corp HUD palette: each corp's UI tinted in their color. Cheap, readable, screams "asymmetric."
  • Fertilizer loop visualization: when biological waste returns to agri, show a small circular indicator on the world map — players see the loop close.
  • Heat-map system is reusable: same overlay infrastructure later shows pollution, power coverage, water access, even market reach for business.
  • Field-tending minigame might double as an idle-progress meter — when you're not interacting, it slowly drifts toward bad pH; tending pulls it back up.
